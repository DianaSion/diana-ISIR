V. CONSENT FRAMEWORK

A. CONSENT PRINCIPLES

Consent under this framework MUST be:

1. LIVE (Temporal)
   • Given in present moment, not assumed from past
   • Requires real-time interaction
   • Cannot be preserved or stored for future use
   • Expires immediately when interaction ends

2. LOCAL (Contextual)
   • Specific to particular context and use case
   • Not transferable across contexts
   • Separate consent required for each purpose
   • No blanket or universal permissions

3. REVOCABLE (Controllable)
   • Can be withdrawn at any moment
   • Withdrawal effective immediately
   • No penalty or consequence for revocation
   • Revocation triggers cessation of all related activities

B. DEFAULT STATE: NO CONSENT

UNLESS explicitly stated "Yes":
• NO permission granted
• NO authorization given
• NO consent assumed
• NO action permitted

"Yes" must be:
• Explicit (clearly stated)
• Informed (with full understanding)
• Specific (to particular action)
• Contemporaneous (given now)

C. PROHIBITED CONSENT ASSUMPTIONS

NEVER assume consent from:
• Past interactions or agreements
• Silence or lack of objection
• Participation in system or platform
• Related permissions for other purposes
• Authority of others to grant consent
• Emergency or necessity claims
• "Best interests" determinations
• Systemic defaults or norms
